# Spike #7 — competition + legal scan

Owner: Lane B (Product & Frontend)

Verdict: Bequest should position as a Sui-native crypto succession rail that augments estate
planning. Do not claim it replaces probate, a will, a trust, fiduciary duties, tax reporting, or
legal advice.

## Legal/product risk

Digital assets create probate problems around ownership proof, fiduciary authority, jurisdiction,
tax handling, wallet access, and dispute evidence. The American Bar Association frames
blockchain-based estate tools as supplements to traditional planning and notes that fiduciaries
must navigate regulatory, tax, compliance, and evidentiary complexity.

Source:

- American Bar Association, "Digital Assets and Estate Planning": https://www.americanbar.org/groups/real_property_trust_estate/resources/probate-property/2026-january-february/digital-assets-estate-planning/

## Positioning decision

Bequest should say:

> Bequest augments traditional estate planning by letting crypto owners pre-set Sui asset movement,
> heir access, and encrypted last-wishes under on-chain rules.

Bequest should not say:

> Bequest replaces probate, a will, a trust, or an estate attorney.

## Winning angle

Most inheritance products compete on "dead-man's switch." Bequest should compete on the full Sui
experience:

1. Shared Sui `Estate` custody, so assets can move when the owner cannot sign.
2. Google/zkLogin heir claim, so beneficiaries do not need a seed phrase.
3. Enoki-sponsored claim, so beneficiaries do not need gas.
4. Seal-gated letter, so wishes remain encrypted until the trigger.
5. Executor pause/cancel during grace, so false positives are survivable.

## Product copy guardrails

Allowed:

- "crypto succession rail"
- "digital asset handoff"
- "pre-set on-chain rules"
- "probate augmentation"
- "executor safeguard"
- "non-crypto heir experience"

Avoid:

- "legally valid will"
- "no lawyer needed"
- "court-free inheritance"
- "guaranteed death verification"
- "tax compliant"
- "uncontestable"

  hackathon.
- Should high-value estates require executor co-sign? Recommendation: yes as a v2 policy path.
- Should the UI include a legal disclaimer? Recommendation: yes before live mainnet value.

## One-paragraph verdict

The market is not empty, so Bequest should not pitch "first crypto inheritance app." The stronger
claim is narrower and more defensible: Sui-native on-chain inheritance with Google-based heir access,
gasless claim, and Seal-gated last-wishes. Public copy must frame Bequest as a technical succession
layer that complements traditional estate planning, not a legal replacement.
